Halo-Halo

The pride of Tiwi, Albay, this halo-halo highlights local ingredients like pili nuts, coconut strips, ube halaya, and sweetened fruits.


Ingredients

Base: Shaved ice, evaporated milk, and sugar.
Mixture: Sweetened beans (munggo, kidney beans), macapuno (coconut sport), nata de coco, kaong (sugar palm fruit), ube halaya, and langka (jackfruit).
Toppings: Leche flan, pinipig (pounded rice), and a scoop of ube or vanilla ice cream.

Procedures

1. Place sweetened fruits and beans at the bottom of a glass.

2. Add a generous amount of finely shaved ice.

3. Drizzle evaporated milk over the ice.

4. Add a slice of leche flan and a scoop of ice cream.

5. Provide a long spoon for mixing thoroughly.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 2–4
Difficulty: Easy

Pili Nut Brittle

The pili nut, one of Bicol’s most treasured products, is known for its buttery texture and rich flavor.


Ingredients

Star Ingredient: Whole or chopped Pili nuts.
The Glaze: White or brown sugar, a small amount of water, and sometimes a touch of butter or vanilla for richness.

Procedures

1. Roast pili nuts until crunchy and golden.

2. Heat sugar and water until it turns into amber caramel.

3. Stir the toasted nuts into the hot syrup.

4. Pour the mixture onto a greased flat surface.

5. Let it harden completely, then break into pieces.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3–5
Difficulty: Medium

SIli Ice Cream

Sili Ice Cream balances creamy coconut milk with local siling labuyo for a sweet, fiery bite.


Ingredients

Dairy Base: Heavy cream, condensed milk, and evaporated milk.
The Heat: Blended Siling Labuyo (bird's eye chili).
The Flavor: Often has a hint of vanilla or a strawberry-like base to balance the spice.

Procedures

1. Blend siling labuyo with a small amount of cream. Strain if you want a
smooth texture, or leave bits for a visual pop.

2. Whip heavy cream until stiff peaks form.

3. Gently fold in condensed milk and the chili infusion.

4. Pour into a container and freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3–5
Difficulty: Medium

Pinangat

Pinangat is similar to laing but uses whole taro leaves wrapped around fish, shrimp, or meat, tied neatly, then simmered in coconut milk.


Ingredients

Wrapper: Fresh Gabi (taro) leaves.
Filling: Shredded young coconut meat (lukadon), ginger, shrimp paste (bagoong), and sometimes tiny shrimp or smoked fish (tinapa).
Liquid: Thick coconut cream (unang piga) flavored with lemongrass and garlic.

Procedures

1. Combine shredded coconut, ginger, and shrimp paste.

2. Place filling inside layered gabi leaves and tie securely.

3. Arrange pouches in a pot over a bed of lemongrass.

4. Pour coconut milk and simmer until the leaves are tender.

5. Cook until the sauce is thick and oily.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3–5
Difficulty: Medium

Bicol Express

This creamy stew of pork, coconut milk, bagoong, and chilies remains one of the country’s best-loved dishes.


Ingredients

Proteins: Pork belly (cut into small cubes) and shrimp paste (bagoong alamang).
The Kick: Large amounts of sliced siling haba (long green peppers) and siling labuyo.
Liquid: Heavy coconut milk and coconut cream.

Procedures

1. Sauté garlic, onions, and ginger. Add the
pork belly and cook until lightly browned.

2. Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook
for a minute to release the aroma.

3. Add the coconut milk and the abundance of sliced chilies.

4. Let it simmer over low heat. The goal is for
the liquid to evaporate until only a thick, creamy sauce (and the rendered pork fat) remains.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3–5
Difficulty: Medium

Santolan

The result is creamy, sour, and lightly spicy; a unique take on fruit-based cooking in Bicol food culture.


Ingredients

Sauté: Sauté garlic and onions, then add minced pork until browned.
Add the squeezed, grated santol and the shrimp paste. Stir well.
Finish: Simmer until the santol has absorbed the coconut milk and the dish becomes dry and oily (nagmamantika).

Procedures

1. Sauté garlic, onions, and minced pork.

2. Mix in grated santol and shrimp paste.

3. Add coconut milk and sliced chilies.

4. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally.

5. Wait until the liquid reduces and the oil separates.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 10 mins
Cook Time: 20 mins
Servings: 3–5
Difficulty: Medium
